Understanding Bonds

Bonds are debt instruments issued by governments and corporations to raise capital. Investors lend money to these entities in exchange for periodic interest payments.

How Bonds Work

When you buy a bond, you essentially become a lender. The issuer promises to pay back the principal at maturity and provides interest payments (coupon) along the way.

Bond Risks

  • Interest Rate Risk
  • Credit Risk
  • Liquidity Risk
  • Inflation Risk
